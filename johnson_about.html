About Rebekah Johnson

Biography Me Resume Trivia

As a Harrisburg Area Community College Graduate, Rebekah Johnson is looking forward to entering the workforce. While studying to get her AAS in Web Development and Design, Rebekah has experience with HTML, CSS, SQL, C#, JavaScript, and products in the Adobe Creative Cloud. Social media literacy and being able to adapt to new applications are important technology skills Rebekah has.

She has worked at her local Sheetz for three years. She enjoyed working with her coworkers to create a clean and fully-stocked environment for full customer satisfaction. She has been described as an excellent team player. Through her experience as a third shift supervisor, She learned the importance of communication, a positive attitude, and keeping a cool head. Running a store taught her to come up with creative solutions to problems that may arise during a shift. It also was a good lesson in balancing and managing workloads. She attended college and balanced a third shift supervisor job, and elected to demote in order to focus on her last year of college. She has worked both first and third shift as a regular employee.

I graduated New Oxford High School in 2018. My favorite classes and activities were drafting, 3d art, computer programming, and music, particularly the yearly musicals. I worked with autocad, and also built floorplans and building designs. When I went to HACC i took an architecture class with John Boecker. It was more design oriented, and I feel I learned a new aspect of architecture i hadnt thought of before. While I had a lot of fun, unfortunately I did not feel it was the path for me.

In 3d art I learned how to make all sorts of things in all sorts of mediums. As a freshman, I entered the class early on recommendation from the middle school art teacher, and met a senior who took me under her wing. She helped me get acquainted with more traditional art concepts, and we helped each other with each of our projects. I continued to the next class, and learned how to make more things. I took a 3d art class at HACC as well, and I enjoyed it, although I struggled sometimes with reaching outside of the box.

However, at the end I felt most accomplished when I was praised by the professor for understanding the final bonus assignment. We were to make art that gelled with nature, while not completely disturbing it. He showed us a video of someone who piled driftwood into an igloo-like structure, and watched as the tides came in and pulled it all back out. People made things like wreaths and painted on leaves, or put rocks in their backyards. I did something I thought was unusual, and took helicopter seeds from under an oak tree, and found a spot of soil with cracks in the ground. I put the seeds in the ground, so the wings were above the soil, but the seed planted below. I took photos on the ground, as if the wings were trees. I also took one from standing level, displaying how unassuming it was from a normal human's vision. At the time I wasn't sure of myself, but I was reassured when the professor was excited to see my unusual piece.

In high school programming, we learned java and javascript. I enjoyed this class a lot, and sped ahead of my classmates, although I did take it a year later than everyone else starting out (same as my spanish classes). I built a lot of complex things in the programs we were using (detailed animations in ALICE, I built an entire treasure planet ship in a browser).

I participated in every musical in high school and county choir, and learned a lot about the dedication to a project and the subsequent pride of presenting my hard work. I was responsible for balancing homework and practice. I had leading roles in my last two musical performances, and the latter was surprising, as no other senior had gotten a lead role and I was able to work hard and put in the time. I also adapted to a whole new team, as newcomers came in and seniors graduated, I acted as a smiling face and ambassador. I was awarded the most improved in my grade by my choir teacher, and was part of the choir council. I also did women's choir and indoor percussion my last year.

I live at home with my parents along with my two younger brothers. We have lived in New Oxford, PA for 14 years now. We currently have 10 cats, a dog, and a bunch of chickens.

My fiance and I have been engaged for a few months now, and are still planning out things. We have been dating since 2015. He graduated from Penn State last year, and is an engineer.
